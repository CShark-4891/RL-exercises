Week 3 – Level 2 Observations (SARSA vs Q-learning)

Both SARSA and Q-learning were tested on the MarsRover environment using identical settings. 
Initially, both agents achieved the same final performance (return = 90.0), which indicated a configuration issue. 
After explicitly selecting the algorithm, a clear difference emerged: 
SARSA maintained a return of 90.0, while Q-learning dropped to 50.0.

Analyzing the learned Q-values and greedy policies showed that both agents preferred moving right in most states. 
However, in the final state (s4), SARSA continued choosing the right action and remained in the high-reward region, 
resulting in a stable trajectory and consistently high returns. 
In contrast, Q-learning learned to prefer the left action in s4, causing the agent to oscillate between states 3 and 4, 
which led to lower overall returns.

This difference can be explained by the learning strategies: SARSA, being on-policy, 
updates its values based on the actions it actually takes, leading to more stable and conservative behavior. 
Q-learning, as an off-policy method, updates using the maximum possible future reward, which can lead to overestimation. 
In this case, it overvalued a suboptimal action, resulting in unstable behavior and a worse policy.

In conclusion, while both methods can learn effective policies, SARSA proved more stable in this environment, 
whereas Q-learning exhibited over-optimistic estimates that led to suboptimal behavior.
