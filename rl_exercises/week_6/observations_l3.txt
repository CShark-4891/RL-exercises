Week 6 - Level 3 observations
=============================

Setup
-----
We implemented Soft Actor-Critic and compared it against the Week 6 PPO agent
on Pendulum-v1.

Pendulum-v1 has a continuous one-dimensional torque action in [-2, 2].  SAC can
act in this native continuous action space.  The PPO implementation from Level
2 uses a categorical policy and therefore needs a discrete action space, so the
PPO baseline uses a wrapper with 9 evenly spaced torque actions.

The comparison is therefore not perfectly symmetric:
- SAC: native continuous Pendulum-v1 action space
- PPO: Pendulum-v1 with discretized torque actions

Both agents used seeds 0 and 1, trained for 3000 environment steps, and were
evaluated every 1000 steps with three deterministic evaluation episodes.

SAC implementation
------------------
The SAC implementation contains the main algorithmic pieces:
- a squashed Gaussian actor for bounded continuous actions;
- two Q-functions and slowly updated target Q-functions;
- an off-policy replay buffer;
- entropy-regularized policy updates;
- automatic entropy-temperature tuning with alpha.

The actor samples actions with the reparameterization trick.  The tanh
squashing correction is included in the log-probability calculation, which is
important because the policy distribution changes after the tanh transform.

Observed results
----------------
Final evaluation means after 3000 steps:
- PPO with discretized actions: -1432.6
- SAC with continuous actions: -1178.8

Neither agent solved Pendulum in this small run, but SAC was clearly better at
the final evaluation point.  SAC improved strongly between 2000 and 3000 steps,
while PPO with discretized actions stayed more noisy.

This result fits the expected sample-efficiency profile.  SAC is off-policy and
can reuse transitions from the replay buffer many times.  PPO is on-policy and
throws away old trajectory data after the update.  On a continuous-control task
with only 3000 environment steps, replay is a major advantage.

Paper discussion
----------------
The SAC paper argues that maximizing both reward and entropy gives more robust
exploration and avoids premature convergence to a brittle deterministic policy.
We can partially see that in the implementation: the actor is stochastic and
the alpha temperature controls the entropy-reward trade-off automatically.

The paper also emphasizes sample efficiency.  Our short Pendulum run supports
this directionally: SAC reached a better final return than the on-policy PPO
baseline under the same step budget.

What we cannot verify from this small exercise run is the full asymptotic claim
of the paper.  The original SAC results use longer training, more seeds, and
larger continuous-control benchmarks.  Our run is enough to show the algorithmic
difference, but not enough to claim that SAC has converged.

Conclusion
----------
SAC is a better fit for Pendulum-v1 than the categorical PPO implementation
because it works directly in the continuous action space and reuses experience
off-policy.  In the short two-seed run, SAC achieved a better final evaluation
return than PPO with discretized actions.
