Week 4 - Level 2 observations
=============================

Setup
-----
We trained the base DQN on CartPole-v1 for 20000 frames.
For Level 2 we used five seeds: 0, 1, 2, 3, and 4.
We kept the DQN hyperparameters fixed for all seeds.

The base DQN setup was:
- hidden_dim=64
- hidden_layers=2
- buffer_capacity=10000
- batch_size=32
- learning_rate=0.001
- gamma=0.99
- epsilon_start=1.0
- epsilon_final=0.01
- epsilon_decay=500
- target_update_freq=1000

Why we used this setup
----------------------
In Level 2 we did not compare different hyperparameters.
We wanted to test how much the same DQN setup changes when only the random seed
changes.

Each seed changes random network initialization, exploration, and sampled replay
batches.  This is important in Deep RL because one lucky or unlucky seed can
make an algorithm look much better or worse than it really is.

Observed results
----------------
The final rewards were quite different across seeds:
- seed 0: final=294.0, best=306.2 at 10000 frames
- seed 1: final=133.9, best=228.5 at 16000 frames
- seed 2: final=138.8, best=192.2 at 15000 frames
- seed 3: final=205.9, best=314.4 at 9000 frames
- seed 4: final=243.4, best=243.4 at 20000 frames

The final mean reward across seeds was 203.2.
The final median reward was 205.9.
The final IQM was about 196.0.
The final rewards ranged from 133.9 to 294.0.

The mean training curve reached its best value at 16000 frames with 232.4.
At the end, at 20000 frames, the mean was lower with 203.2.
This shows that the agent did not improve monotonically for every seed.

RLiable interpretation
----------------------
Plain averages are useful, but they hide how different the seeds are.
For example, seed 0 looked much better than seed 1 and seed 2 at the end.
If we only reported seed 0, DQN would look much stronger than it looked on
average.

RLiable makes the result more robust because it reports several statistics:
- Mean: the normal average over seeds
- Median: the middle seed result
- IQM: a trimmed mean that is less affected by extreme seeds
- Optimality gap: how far the normalized score is from the target score

We feel more confident with the RLiable plot than with one seed only.
However, five seeds are still not enough to make strong claims about small
differences.  The result is more reliable than one run, but it is still noisy.

Conclusion
----------
Level 2 shows that DQN on CartPole is sensitive to random seeds.
The same hyperparameters produced final rewards from 133.9 to 294.0.
Therefore, reporting only one run would be misleading.

Using RLiable gives a better summary because it shows robust metrics and
uncertainty across seeds.  We would trust these results more than a single
training curve.
