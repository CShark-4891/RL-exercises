Week 4 - Level 3 observations
=============================

Setup
-----
We trained DQN on CartPole-v1 for 20000 frames.
For Level 3 we used five seeds: 0, 1, 2, 3, and 4.
We compared four DQN variants:
- base_dqn
- prioritized_replay
- double_dqn
- prioritized_replay_double_dqn

All variants used the same basic hyperparameters:
- hidden_dim=64
- hidden_layers=2
- buffer_capacity=10000
- batch_size=32
- learning_rate=0.001
- gamma=0.99
- epsilon_start=1.0
- epsilon_final=0.01
- epsilon_decay=500
- target_update_freq=1000

What the variants change
------------------------
The base DQN uses uniform replay and the normal DQN target.

Prioritized replay changes the replay buffer.  Instead of sampling all
transitions with the same probability, transitions with a larger TD error are
sampled more often.  The idea is that surprising transitions can be more useful
for learning.

Double DQN changes the target calculation.  The online network chooses the best
next action, and the target network evaluates this action.  This reduces the
overestimation problem of normal DQN.

The combined variant uses both prioritized replay and Double DQN.

Paper idea
----------
The Rainbow paper combines several improvements to DQN.  It asks if these
individual improvements work well together.  The full Rainbow algorithm includes
more components than we implemented, but in this task we focused on two common
ones: prioritized replay and Double DQN.

Observed final results
----------------------
The final results across five seeds were:
- base_dqn: mean=203.2, median=205.9, IQM=196.0
- prioritized_replay: mean=228.7, median=250.0, IQM=232.0
- double_dqn: mean=215.8, median=225.1, IQM=223.5
- prioritized_replay_double_dqn: mean=163.1, median=151.8, IQM=166.2

The best mean curve values during training were:
- base_dqn: 232.4 at 16000 frames
- prioritized_replay: 276.0 at 9000 frames
- double_dqn: 238.2 at 15000 frames
- prioritized_replay_double_dqn: 251.5 at 10000 frames

Interpretation
--------------
Prioritized replay performed best in this run.  It had the highest final mean,
highest final median, and highest final IQM.  It also reached its best mean
performance earlier than the base DQN.  This suggests that replaying high-error
transitions helped sample efficiency on CartPole.

Double DQN was also better than base DQN at the final frame.  It had a higher
mean, median, and IQM than base DQN.  This suggests that reducing overestimation
helped the agent learn more stable values.

The combined variant was not the best in our run.  It reached a good mean value
around 10000 frames, but its final mean dropped to 163.1.  This shows that adding
two improvements does not automatically make the result better in a small
environment like CartPole.

RLiable interpretation
----------------------
The RLiable plot is useful because the results are noisy across seeds.
For example, one variant can have a high best value in one seed, but still have
a weaker final average across all seeds.

IQM is helpful here because it is less affected by one very good or very bad
seed.  In our final results, prioritized replay had the strongest IQM, followed
by Double DQN, base DQN, and then the combined variant.

Conclusion
----------
In our Level 3 ablation, prioritized replay was the strongest variant.
Double DQN also improved over base DQN.
The combined variant did not improve the final result in this CartPole run.

This matches the main lesson of the Rainbow paper in a small way: DQN
improvements can help, but their effect should be tested with ablations and
multiple seeds instead of assumed.
